Our three-year data strategy has three pillars, and the order matters. Pillar one is the Unified Data Platform — you cannot do ML or analytics well if your data is scattered across 15 different systems. We are consolidating onto a lakehouse architecture with governed access. Pillar two is Embedded Intelligence — once we have the platform, we embed ML models directly into business workflows: pricing decisions, customer routing, inventory optimization. Pillar three is the hardest: building a Data-Driven Culture. Technology alone is not enough. We need training programs, decision frameworks, and leadership accountability to make data the default input for decisions. Each pillar reinforces the others — the platform enables the intelligence, the intelligence demonstrates the value, and the value drives the culture change.
Four capabilities that make our ML platform production-ready
Feature Store
Centralized, versioned feature repository with point-in-time correctness and sub-10ms online serving
FEAST + REDIS
Experiment Tracking
Every training run logged with hyperparameters, metrics, artifacts, and reproducible environment snapshots
MLFLOW
Auto-Retraining
Drift-triggered retraining pipelines with automated validation gates and rollback capability
AIRFLOW + K8S
Production Monitoring
Real-time dashboards for prediction distribution, latency percentiles, and data quality SLAs
EVIDENTLY + GRAFANA
These are the four pillars of our ML platform. The Feature Store, built on Feast with a Redis online store, gives every model team a shared, versioned feature repository — no more duplicating feature engineering logic across teams. Experiment Tracking through MLflow ensures every training run is reproducible: we log hyperparameters, metrics, data snapshots, and full environment specs. Auto-Retraining is the automation layer — when our monitoring detects data drift above threshold, it triggers a retraining pipeline with automated validation gates. If the new model does not beat the incumbent on our test suite, it does not deploy. And Production Monitoring via Evidently and Grafana gives us real-time visibility into prediction distributions, latency, and data quality. Together, these four capabilities reduced our model deployment time from 3 weeks to 2 days.
