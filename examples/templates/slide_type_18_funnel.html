This funnel visualizes how data flows through our prediction pipeline, with attrition at each stage. We start with 10 million raw rows. After data cleaning — removing duplicates, null values, and invalid records — we're down to 8.2 million, an 18% drop. Feature engineering further reduces this to 6.1 million because some records lack the required fields for feature computation. The training set is 4.8 million after applying our temporal train-test split and removing data leakage candidates. Finally, only 3.2 million rows produce valid predictions — those that pass our confidence threshold of 0.7. The biggest drop-off is at the prediction confidence gate at 33%. We should investigate whether lowering the threshold to 0.6 would meaningfully increase coverage without degrading precision.

This horizontal funnel tracks how requests flow through our model serving pipeline. We receive 500,000 daily API requests. 84% pass input validation, dropping to 420,000 valid inputs. Of those, 320,000 successfully complete model inference — the 24% drop comes from timeouts and feature store cache misses. The biggest bottleneck is the confidence gate: only 56% of inferences produce high-confidence results above our 0.75 threshold, yielding 180,000 predictions. Finally, 64% of those are actioned by downstream systems, giving us 115,000 end-to-end completions or a 23% overall conversion rate. The model inference to high-confidence drop is our biggest opportunity — improving model accuracy at the decision boundary could recover tens of thousands of daily predictions.

This side-by-side funnel comparison tells the story of our pipeline migration. On the left, the legacy batch pipeline v1 started with 5 million records but retained only 22% through to scoring — 1.1 million records. The biggest loss was at validation where 40% of data was dropped due to rigid schema checks. On the right, the new streaming pipeline v2 starts from the same 5 million but retains 52% through to scoring — 2.6 million records. The validation stage now passes 88% of data thanks to flexible schema evolution and better null handling. Feature engineering retention improved from 36% to 72% because we added fallback feature computation for incomplete records. Overall, we get 2.4 times more scored records, a 30 percentage point improvement in end-to-end retention, and a 47% improvement in validation pass rate. This directly translates to better model coverage and more revenue.
