Model v3.2 catches 94% of fraudulent transactions while keeping false positives below 1.4%

By rebalancing the training set with SMOTE and tuning the classification threshold to 0.42, we achieved a recall of 0.941 without significantly degrading precision.

94.1% Recall (sensitivity)
87.2% Precision
The confusion matrix on the left tells the story clearly. Out of 900 actual fraud cases, our model correctly flagged 847 — that's a recall of 94.1%. The 53 missed cases is our primary area for improvement. On the false positive side, only 124 out of over 9,000 legitimate transactions were incorrectly flagged. This 1.4% false positive rate is well within our operational tolerance — the fraud review team can handle that volume without customer experience degradation.

Model Comparison

Gradient boosting consistently outperforms neural networks on tabular datasets under 100K rows

Model AUC-ROC Train Time
XGBoost 0.934 12 min
LightGBM 0.929 8 min
MLP (3-layer) 0.891 47 min
TabNet 0.903 62 min
This comparison table summarizes our benchmark across four model families. XGBoost led on AUC-ROC at 0.934, trained in just 12 minutes. The neural approaches — MLP and TabNet — both underperformed on this dataset size. This aligns with recent research: for structured tabular data with fewer than 100,000 rows, tree-based methods still reign. We chose XGBoost as our production model for this reason.
