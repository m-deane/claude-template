On Production ML Systems

The gap between a proof-of-concept model and a production-grade system is not technical — it is organizational. You need monitoring, rollback plans, and a team that treats model failures like software incidents.
MR

Martin Rinard

VP of ML Engineering, Stripe

Martin's perspective is shaped by years of running ML systems that process billions of dollars in transactions. His point about treating model failures like software incidents changed how our team operates. We now have on-call rotations for model performance, automated alerts when prediction distributions shift, and runbooks for every production model. The technical part of ML is the easy part — the operational discipline is what separates research from production.
We stopped asking 'what is the most accurate model?' and started asking 'what is the simplest model our team can maintain and improve every week?' That shift tripled our deployment velocity.
KW

Kira Watanabe

Director of Data Science, Shopify
MLOps Summit 2026

Kira's team manages 40+ production models serving real-time predictions across Shopify's e-commerce platform, processing over 100 million inference requests daily.

Kira's team at Shopify went through the same evolution we did. They had a sophisticated ensemble model that scored two points higher on their primary metric — but it took three engineers two weeks to update. When they replaced it with a simpler gradient boosting model, they could iterate weekly. The cumulative improvement from rapid iteration far outpaced the initial accuracy advantage of the complex model. This is the mindset shift we're advocating for: optimize for iteration speed, not peak benchmark performance.
